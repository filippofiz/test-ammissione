*Question*:
A company surveyed its managers and employees to determine how important flexibility is to them and whether they use any of the company's flexible offerings themselves.

Based on the information provided, select from each drop-down menu the option that creates the most accurate statement.

*Blank 1* are the group most likely to change their schedule.
*Blank 2* are the group least likely to telecommute and the group least likely to work a part-time schedule.

*Options for Blank 1*:
- Managers
- Salaried employees
- Hourly employees

*Options for Blank 2*:
- Managers
- Salaried employees
- Hourly employees

*Answer*:
Blank 1: Managers
Blank 2: Managers

*Explanation*:
**Recognize**
As a preliminary observation, note that the graph label say flexibility is important is the only label that pertains to "Importance" in the graph title, while each of the three remaining labels pertains to "Use of Flexibility" in the graph title. These three remaining labels specify the three types of flexibility that the graph gives information about.
**RO1**
The only graph label that pertains to "change of schedule" is use change of schedule. There are three columns above this label. Each of the column heights corresponds to the percentage of employees in a single job type that uses change of schedule. The job type that has the greatest percentage of its members using change of schedule corresponds to the column having the greatest height, which is "manager" (black column). Therefore, "Managers" is the group whose members are most likely to change their schedule. **The correct answer is Managers.**
**RO2**
The graph labels that pertain to "telecommute" and "part-time schedule" are telecommute and use part-time scheduling. For each of these, "manager" (black column) is the job type that has the least percentage of its members using the corresponding type of flexibility. Therefore, "Managers" is the group whose members are least likely to telecommute
and the group whose members are least likely to work a part-time schedule. **The correct answer is Managers.**

*Question*:
Four students from Mistville and four from Fogtown will attend school in New York City this year, and each group will rent an apartment.
- Type $A$ apartments are each $1,390$ square feet, have $2$ bedrooms, a $10$-by-$10$-foot kitchen, and are $4$ miles from the school. The price per square foot per month is $\$1.94$.
- Type $B$ apartments are each $1,250$ square feet, have $3$ bedrooms, an $11$-by-$11$-foot kitchen, and are $3$ miles from the school. The rent is $\$2,250$ per month.
- Type $C$ apartments are each $1,210$ square feet, have $2$ bedrooms, a $7$-by-$9$-foot kitchen, and are $4.5$ miles from the school. The rent is $\$2,190$ per month.
The Mistville students choose a type $C$ apartment and the Fogtown students choose a type $A$ apartment.

Assume, for each group of students, that the group's choice of apartment type was based solely on an accurate assessment, using only the information above, of which apartment type met a particular criterion listed in the table.

| **Mistville students** | **Fogtown students** | |
|:---:|:---:|:---|
| ○ | ○ | Lowest rent |
| ○ | ○ | Largest kitchen (square footage) |
| ○ | ○ | Greatest number of bedrooms |
| ○ | ○ | Largest total square footage |
| ○ | ○ | Fewest miles from school |
| ○ | ○ | Lowest price per square foot |

*Answers*:
- **Mistville students**: Lowest rent (Row 1)
- **Fogtown students**: Largest total square footage (Row 4)

*Explanation*:
**Evaluate**
The table below shows, for each of the six apartment attributes under consideration, the numerical values associated with that attribute for the three types of apartments. The numerical values that satisfy the stated criterion for an attribute are given in bold font. All but three of these values can be quickly obtained from the given information.
The three exceptions are the monthly rent for type $A$, which is ($1,390\, ft^2$)($\$1.94/ft^2$) = $\$2,696.60$, and the monthly price per square foot for type $B$ and type $C$ apartments, each of which can be obtained by dividing the monthly rent by the square footage. Incidentally, both column selections can be made without having to calculate the price per square foot values, because each of the type $A$ and type $C$ apartments already satisfy one of the other criteria.
| **Attribute** | **Criterion** | **$A$ (Fogtown)** | **$B$** | **$C$ (Mistville)** |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Rent | least | $2,696.60 | $2,250 | **$2,190** |
| Kitchen | most | 100 ft$^2$ | **121 ft$^2$** | 63 ft$^2$ |
| Bedrooms | most | 2 | **3** | 2 |
| Footage | most | **1,390** | 1,250 | 1,210 |
| Miles | least | 4 | **3** | 4.5 |
| Price/ft$^2$ | least | $1.94 | **$1.80** | $1.81 |
**RO1**
The Mistville group chose a type $C$ apartment, and only one of the values under "$C$ (Mistville)" in the table above is bolded. Thus, for type $C$ apartments, the only attribute that meets a criterion is the monthly rent of the
apartment. Given that the Mistville group chose a type $C$ apartment based only on meeting a particular criterion listed in the table, it follows that the Mistville group's criterion was having the lowest rent. **The correct answer is Lowest rent.**
**RO2**
The Fogtown group chose a type $A$ apartment, and only one of the values under "$A$ (Fogtown)" in the table above is bolded. Thus, for type $A$ apartments, the only attribute that meets a criterion is the square footage of the
apartment. Given that the Fogtown group chose a type $A$ apartment based only on meeting a particular criterion listed in the table, it follows that the Fogtown group's criterion was having the largest total square footage.
The correct answer is Largest total square footage.
**Tip**: When solving this problem, it is not necessary to fill out a table as shown above because there are various observations that can lead to a quicker solution. However, given the variety of such observations and the fact
that their use depends on recognizing them, the solution above seems best for general use. As an example of a quicker solution, if it is noticed that the greatest of the first-listed attributes (total square footage) corresponds
to type $A$ apartments, then one can immediately choose "Largest total square footage" for Fogtown students. It is now easy to eliminate all but "Lowest rent" and "Lowest price per square foot" for Mistville students. Now
calculate $(1,390)(1.94)$ to see whether type $C$ apartments have the lowest rent (choose "Lowest rent" for Mistville) or type $C$ apartments do not have the lowest rent (choose "Lowest price per square foot" for Mistville).
Moreover, since $(1,390)(1.94)$ is a little less than twice 1,390, it follows that $(1,390)(1.94)$ cannot be less than $2,190$. Thus, we can conclude that type $C$ apartments have the lowest rent without carrying out the multiplication
for $(1,390)(1.94)$.
