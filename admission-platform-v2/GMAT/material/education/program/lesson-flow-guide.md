# Lesson Flow Guide

## Overview

Every lesson follows a consistent 90-minute format. The number of lessons per topic varies by cycle:

| Cycle | Lessons per Topic | Focus |
|-------|-------------------|-------|
| Foundation | 4 (A, B, C, D) | Building fundamentals with extensive review |
| Development | 3 (A, B, C) | Balanced instruction and practice |
| Excellence | 2 (A, B) | Condensed delivery with autonomous work |

---

# Cycle 1: Foundation (4 Lessons per Topic)

Students in the Foundation cycle need more time to absorb concepts and receive feedback. The Quick Test is completed as homework after Lesson A, with review in Lesson B.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        LESSON A (90 min)                        │
│  Frontal Lesson (55 min) → Guided Practice (30 min)            │
│  → Assign Quick Test (homework)                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                              ↓
                    [Student: Quick Test]
                    (5 practice + 5 theory)
                       ~20 min homework
                              ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        LESSON B (90 min)                        │
│  Quick Test Review (25 min) → Deep Concepts (40 min)           │
│  → Question Practice (20 min) → Assign Training #1             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                              ↓
                    [Student: Training #1]
                      (30-45 min, timed)
                              ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        LESSON C (90 min)                        │
│  Training #1 Review (35 min) → Advanced Strategies (35 min)    │
│  → Additional Practice (15 min) → Assign Training #2           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                              ↓
                    [Student: Training #2]
                      (30-45 min, timed)
                              ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        LESSON D (90 min)                        │
│  Training #2 Review (25 min) → Topic Assessment (45 min)       │
│  → Assessment Review (15 min) → Transition                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Foundation - Lesson A: Introduction

### Purpose
Introduce the topic and core concepts. Assign Quick Test for homework to verify understanding.

### Time Breakdown (90 minutes)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Opening | 5 min | Preview topic, connect to overall section |
| Frontal Lesson | 50 min | Present concepts using lesson material |
| Break | 5 min | Short break |
| Guided Practice | 25 min | Work through 5-8 example questions together |
| Wrap-up | 5 min | Summarize, assign Quick Test |

### Frontal Lesson Guidelines

1. **Introduction** (5 min): What is this topic? Why is it important for GMAT?
2. **Core Concepts** (25 min): Key definitions, formulas, rules - take time to ensure understanding
3. **Basic Strategies** (15 min): How to approach these questions on the GMAT
4. **Common Traps** (5 min): What mistakes to avoid

### Assigning Quick Test
- Explain format: 5 practice questions + 5 theory questions
- Theory questions require written answers (definitions, explanations)
- Tutor will evaluate before next lesson
- Not strictly timed, but should take ~20 minutes

---

## Foundation - Lesson B: Quick Test Review & Deep Concepts

### Purpose
Review Quick Test results, identify gaps in understanding, and deepen conceptual knowledge.

### Time Breakdown (90 minutes)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Quick Test Review | 25 min | Review both practice and theory answers |
| Break | 5 min | Short break |
| Deep Concepts | 35 min | Expand on concepts based on Quick Test gaps |
| Question Practice | 20 min | Practice additional questions together |
| Wrap-up | 5 min | Assign Training #1 |

### Quick Test Review Process

#### Practice Questions Review (15 min)
- Review each incorrect answer
- Identify conceptual vs. procedural errors
- Re-solve problems with student

#### Theory Questions Review (10 min)
- Evaluate written definitions and explanations
- Correct misconceptions
- Reinforce key terminology
- Foundation students: accept partial answers, guide to complete understanding

### Assigning Training #1
- 15-20 questions, 30-45 minutes strictly timed
- Emphasize: no pausing, simulate test conditions
- Note which questions were hardest

---

## Foundation - Lesson C: Training Review & Advanced Strategies

### Purpose
Review Training #1, introduce advanced strategies, and assign Training #2.

### Time Breakdown (90 minutes)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Training #1 Review | 35 min | Thorough error analysis |
| Break | 5 min | Short break |
| Advanced Strategies | 30 min | Efficient approaches, pattern recognition |
| Additional Practice | 15 min | 2-3 harder questions together |
| Wrap-up | 5 min | Assign Training #2 |

### Training Review Process
- Categorize errors: Conceptual, Procedural, Strategic, Time-related
- Select 4-6 questions for deep review
- For each: student explains thinking → identify error → demonstrate correct approach
- Foundation students: take extra time on conceptual errors

---

## Foundation - Lesson D: Assessment

### Purpose
Review Training #2, conduct topic assessment, and transition to next topic.

### Time Breakdown (90 minutes)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Training #2 Review | 25 min | Focus on remaining gaps |
| Topic Assessment | 45 min | 20 questions, timed |
| Assessment Review | 15 min | Quick review of results |
| Transition | 5 min | Preview next topic |

### Topic Assessment Administration
- Clear workspace, no notes
- Timer visible to student
- 45 minutes for 20 questions (Foundation pace: ~2.25 min/question)
- Grade together immediately after

                              ↓
                    [Student: Training #1]
                      (30-45 min, timed)
                              ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        LESSON B (90 min)                        │

│  → Practice (20 min) → Assign Training #2                      │

                    [Student: Training #2]

## Development - Lesson A: Introduction & Quick Test

### Purpose
Introduce topic concepts and immediately verify understanding with in-class Quick Test.

### Time Breakdown (90 minutes)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Opening | 5 min | Preview topic |
| Frontal Lesson | 40 min | Present concepts efficiently |
| Quick Test | 20 min | In-class: 5 practice + 5 theory |
| Quick Test Review | 15 min | Immediate feedback |
| Wrap-up | 10 min | Assign Training #1 |

### Quick Test Administration (In-Class)
- Student works independently for 20 minutes
- Tutor observes but does not assist
- Review immediately after
- Development students: expect more complete theory answers

# Cycle 3: Excellence (2 Lessons per Topic)

Excellence students have strong foundations and work more independently. Quick Test is in-class, and Training is combined with Modeling Questions for homework.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        LESSON A (90 min)                        │
│  Condensed Concepts (35 min) → Quick Test in-class (15 min)    │
│  → Review & Strategy (30 min) → Assign Training + Modeling     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                              ↓
                    [Student: Training + Modeling Questions]
                      (60-90 min autonomous work)
                              ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        LESSON B (90 min)                        │
│  Training Review (20 min) → Modeling Review (25 min)           │
│  → Topic Assessment (35 min) → Assessment Review (10 min)      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Excellence - Lesson A: Concepts & Strategy

### Purpose
Efficient concept review with focus on advanced strategies. Assign comprehensive autonomous work.

### Time Breakdown (90 minutes)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Opening | 5 min | Quick topic preview |
| Condensed Concepts | 30 min | Fast-paced concept review (students know basics) |
| Quick Test | 15 min | In-class: 5 practice + 5 theory (harder level) |
| Quick Test Review | 10 min | Brief feedback |
| Advanced Strategy | 20 min | Excellence-level techniques |
| Wrap-up | 10 min | Assign Training + Modeling Questions |

### Assigning Autonomous Work
Excellence students receive:
1. **Training Session**: 15-20 questions, 30-45 min timed
2. **Modeling Questions**: Advanced problem-solving exercises from `exercises-problem-modeling.typ`

Total autonomous work: 60-90 minutes

### Quick Test at Excellence Level
- Theory questions require proofs, derivations, or advanced explanations
- Practice questions are predominantly hard difficulty
- Expect concise, accurate answers

---

## Excellence - Lesson B: Review & Assessment

### Purpose
Review autonomous work including Modeling Questions, then conduct assessment.

### Time Breakdown (90 minutes)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Training Review | 20 min | Focus on remaining weak areas |
| Modeling Review | 25 min | Discuss advanced problem approaches |
| Topic Assessment | 35 min | 20 questions, timed (faster pace) |
| Assessment Review | 10 min | Quick analysis |

### Modeling Questions Review
- Focus on problem-solving approach, not just answers
- Discuss multiple solution methods
- Connect to real GMAT hard questions
- Excellence students should lead discussion

# Quick Test Guidelines (All Cycles)

## Structure
Every Quick Test contains:
- **5 Practice Questions**: GMAT-style problems at cycle-appropriate difficulty
- **5 Theory Questions**: Open-ended conceptual questions

## Theory Question Types by Cycle

### Foundation
- "What is [concept]?"
- "Define [term]."
- "List the steps to solve [problem type]."

Example: "What is a prime number? Give 3 examples."

### Development
- "Explain why [rule] works."
- "Describe the relationship between [A] and [B]."
- "When would you use [strategy]?"

Example: "Explain why 1 is not considered a prime number."

### Excellence
- "Prove that [statement]."
- "Derive the formula for [concept]."
- "Compare and contrast [A] and [B] approaches."

Example: "Derive the formula: GCF(a,b) × LCM(a,b) = a × b"

## Evaluation
- Tutor evaluates all Quick Tests (not auto-graded)
- Practice questions: check correctness and approach
- Theory questions: evaluate completeness and accuracy
- Provide written feedback for Foundation students

---

# Training Session Guidelines (All Cycles)

## Format
- 15-20 questions per session
- 30-45 minutes strictly timed
- No pausing allowed
- Difficulty distribution per cycle (see [question-allocation.md](question-allocation.md))

## Review Process
1. **Score Check**: Calculate percentage, compare to cycle threshold
2. **Error Categorization**:
   - Conceptual (didn't know the concept)
   - Procedural (calculation error)
   - Strategic (wrong approach)
   - Time-related (rushed)
3. **Deep Review**: Select 3-5 questions for detailed analysis
4. **Pattern Identification**: Note recurring error types

# Tutor Best Practices

## Before Each Lesson
- Review previous lesson notes
- Check Quick Test / Training results (if applicable)
- Prepare lesson materials
- Select example questions appropriate for cycle

## During Each Lesson
- Take notes on student performance
- Track time management issues
- Observe confidence levels
- Adapt pace to student needs

## After Each Lesson
- Update student progress record
- Prepare assignments
- Note adjustments for next lesson

## Communication Style
- Encourage without false praise
- Be direct about errors
- Celebrate genuine progress
- Maintain high expectations appropriate to cycle
- Foundation: more patience and repetition
- Development: balance support and challenge
- Excellence: peer-level discussion

---

# Time Management Tips

## Running Short on Time
- Cut the break if needed
- Reduce practice question count
- Assign review of skipped content as reading homework

## Running Long
- Skip less critical examples
- Save advanced content for next lesson
- Don't rush assessments—better to extend if needed

## Adapting by Cycle
- **Foundation**: Take extra time for conceptual explanations
- **Development**: Maintain steady pace, don't over-explain
- **Excellence**: Move quickly, students should ask if unclear
