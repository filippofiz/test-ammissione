# Lesson Flow Guide

## Standard Lesson Structure

Every lesson follows a consistent 90-minute format with flexible time allocation based on content needs.

## The Three-Lesson Cycle

Each vertical topic is covered in exactly 3 lessons:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        LESSON A                                  │
│  Frontal Lesson (50-60 min) → Question Time (30-40 min)         │
│  → Assign Training #1                                            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                              ↓
                    [Student: Training #1]
                      (30-45 min, timed)
                              ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        LESSON B                                  │
│  Review Training #1 (30-40 min) → Deep Dive (30-40 min)         │
│  → Assign Training #2                                            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                              ↓
                    [Student: Training #2]
                      (30-45 min, timed)
                              ↓
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                        LESSON C                                  │
│  Review Training #2 (30-40 min) → Topic Assessment (30-40 min)  │
│  → Assessment Review & Transition                                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Lesson A: Introduction & Fundamentals

### Purpose
Introduce the topic, present core concepts, and begin guided practice.

### Time Breakdown (90 minutes total)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Opening | 5 min | Briefly preview today's topic, connect to overall section |
| Frontal Lesson | 50-55 min | Present concepts using lesson material |
| Break | 5 min | Short break |
| Question Time | 25-30 min | Guided practice with 5-8 example questions |
| Wrap-up | 5 min | Summarize, assign Training #1 |

### Frontal Lesson Guidelines

#### Structure
1. **Introduction** (5 min): What is this topic? Why is it important for GMAT?
2. **Core Concepts** (20-25 min): Key definitions, formulas, rules
3. **Strategies** (15-20 min): How to approach these questions on the GMAT
4. **Common Traps** (10-15 min): What mistakes to avoid

#### Teaching Tips
- Use the lesson material document as your guide
- Pause frequently for questions
- Write key formulas/concepts on screen or whiteboard
- Relate concepts to real-world examples when possible
- Check understanding before moving to next concept

### Question Time Guidelines

#### Selection
- Choose 5-8 questions representing different subtypes
- Start with easier questions, progress to medium
- Include at least 1 question demonstrating common trap

#### Process
1. Display question
2. Give student 1-2 minutes to read and think
3. Ask student to verbalize their approach
4. Guide them if stuck (don't just give answer)
5. Work through solution together
6. Highlight key takeaways

### Assigning Training #1
- Explain the training format (15-20 questions, 30-45 min)
- Emphasize: STRICTLY TIMED, no pausing
- Tell student to note which questions were hardest
- Confirm next lesson time

## Lesson B: Review & Deep Dive

### Purpose
Review training results, address errors, and deepen understanding with advanced strategies.

### Time Breakdown (90 minutes total)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Training #1 Review | 30-40 min | Analyze results, discuss errors |
| Break | 5 min | Short break |
| Deep Dive | 30-35 min | Advanced strategies, complex examples |
| Additional Practice | 10-15 min | 2-3 harder questions together |
| Wrap-up | 5 min | Assign Training #2 |

### Training Review Process

#### Opening (5 min)
- Ask: "How did it go? How did you feel about timing?"
- Get overall score
- Note: Did student finish on time?

#### Error Analysis (25-35 min)
1. **Categorize errors**:
   - Conceptual (didn't know the concept)
   - Procedural (knew concept but made calculation error)
   - Strategic (wrong approach for this question type)
   - Time-related (rushed and made careless error)

2. **Select 3-5 questions for deep review**:
   - Prioritize conceptual errors
   - Include questions student found hardest
   - Mix incorrect and "lucky guesses"

3. **For each reviewed question**:
   - Have student explain their original thinking
   - Identify where they went wrong
   - Demonstrate correct approach
   - Connect to lesson concepts
   - Have student re-solve similar problem

### Deep Dive Content

#### Advanced Strategies
- More efficient approaches
- Pattern recognition techniques
- Time-saving shortcuts
- When to guess and move on

#### Complex Examples
- Multi-step problems
- Questions combining multiple concepts
- Typical "hard" question patterns

### Assigning Training #2
- Same format as Training #1
- May increase difficulty slightly based on Lesson A performance
- Remind about strict timing

## Lesson C: Assessment & Transition

### Purpose
Final review of topic, conduct assessment, and prepare for next topic.

### Time Breakdown (90 minutes total)

| Activity | Duration | Description |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Training #2 Review | 25-30 min | Brief review, focus on remaining gaps |
| Topic Assessment | 40-45 min | 20 questions, timed |
| Assessment Review | 10-15 min | Quick review of results |
| Transition | 5 min | Preview next topic, close lesson |

### Training #2 Review

#### Streamlined Process
- Focus only on persistent error patterns
- Skip questions similar to already-reviewed problems
- Emphasize speed and confidence
- Confirm readiness for assessment

### Topic Assessment Administration

#### Setup
1. Clear workspace, no notes
2. Timer visible to student
3. No interruptions

#### During Assessment
- Student works independently
- Tutor observes silently (notes for later)
- Strict timing (40-45 min for 20 questions)

#### Scoring (immediately after)
- Grade together
- Calculate percentage

### Assessment Review

#### Quick Analysis (10-15 min)
- Note final score
- Identify any major gaps
- Brief review of 1-2 most instructive errors
- Do NOT review all errors (save for next topic's context)

### Transition Decision

#### If PASS (meets cycle threshold)
- Congratulate student
- Preview next topic briefly
- Confirm lesson schedule

#### If NEEDS REVIEW
- Identify specific weak areas
- Plan targeted practice
- May need additional session before moving on

#### If REPEAT TOPIC
- Don't discourage student
- Identify fundamental gaps
- Plan modified Lesson A for same topic
- Use different question set

## Tutor Best Practices

### Before Each Lesson
- Review previous lesson notes
- Check student's training results (if applicable)
- Prepare lesson materials
- Select example questions

### During Each Lesson
- Take notes on student performance
- Note questions that caused difficulty
- Track time management issues
- Observe confidence levels

### After Each Lesson
- Update student progress record
- Prepare training assignment
- Note any follow-ups needed
- Plan adjustments for next lesson

### Communication Style
- Encourage without false praise
- Be direct about errors
- Celebrate genuine progress
- Maintain high expectations
- Make difficult concepts approachable

---

## Time Management Tips

### Running Short on Time
- Cut the break if needed
- Reduce question time examples
- Assign review of skipped content as reading homework

### Running Long
- Skip less critical examples
- Save advanced strategies for Lesson B
- Don't rush the question time—better to do fewer questions well

### Student Pacing Issues
- If student is slow: focus on efficiency strategies
- If student is fast but inaccurate: emphasize careful reading
- Adjust training difficulty based on timing observations
