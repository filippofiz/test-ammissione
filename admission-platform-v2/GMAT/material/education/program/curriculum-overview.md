# UpToTen GMAT Preparation Program - Curriculum Overview

## Program Philosophy

The UpToTen GMAT preparation program is designed to provide structured, personalized tutoring that adapts to each student's starting level and target score. The program follows a cycle-based progression system where students advance through increasingly challenging material as they demonstrate mastery.

## Program Structure

### Section Order
The GMAT preparation follows this sequence:
1. **Quantitative Reasoning (QR)** - Mathematical foundations first
2. **Data Insights (DI)** - Builds on QR skills with data analysis
3. **Verbal Reasoning (VR)** - Last, allowing time for parallel English preparation

### Score Cycles
Students are placed into one of four cycles based on their initial diagnostic:

| Cycle | Name | Target Score | Question Difficulty |
|-------|------|--------------|---------------------|
| 1 | Beginner | 505-555 | 70% Easy, 25% Medium, 5% Hard |
| 2 | Intermediate | 555-615 | 30% Easy, 50% Medium, 20% Hard |
| 3 | Advanced | 615-675 | 10% Easy, 40% Medium, 50% Hard |
| 4 | Elite | 675-715+ | 5% Easy, 25% Medium, 70% Hard |

## Vertical Lessons

Each section is divided into vertical lessons focusing on specific topics:

### Quantitative Reasoning (5 Topics)
1. Number Properties & Arithmetic
2. Algebra
3. Word Problems
4. Statistics & Probability
5. Percents, Ratios & Proportions

### Data Insights (5 Topics)
1. Data Sufficiency (DS)
2. Graphics Interpretation (GI)
3. Table Analysis (TA)
4. Two-Part Analysis (TPA)
5. Multi-Source Reasoning (MSR)

### Verbal Reasoning (2 Topics)
1. Critical Reasoning (CR)
2. Reading Comprehension (RC)

**Total: 12 Vertical Lessons**

## Lesson Flow Per Topic

Each vertical topic follows a 3-lesson structure:

### Lesson A (90 minutes)
- **Frontal Lesson** (50-60 min): Theory presentation, concepts, examples
- **Question Time** (30-40 min): Guided practice with tutor
- **Assignment**: Training #1 (homework)

### Between Lessons
- Student completes **Training #1** at home (30-45 min, strictly timed)

### Lesson B (90 minutes)
- **Review** (30-40 min): Discuss Training #1 results, analyze errors
- **Deep Dive** (30-40 min): Common mistakes, advanced strategies
- **Assignment**: Training #2 (homework)

### Between Lessons
- Student completes **Training #2** at home (30-45 min, strictly timed)

### Lesson C (90 minutes)
- **Review** (30-40 min): Discuss Training #2 results
- **Topic Assessment** (timed, 20 questions)
- **Assessment Review & Transition**: Prepare for next topic

## Timeline Estimates

At 2-3 lessons per week:
- **2 lessons/week**: ~33 weeks per cycle (~8 months)
- **3 lessons/week**: ~22 weeks per cycle (~5.5 months)

Students requiring multiple cycles will need proportionally more time.

## Assessment Checkpoints

1. **Initial Diagnostic**: Full GMAT simulation to determine starting cycle
2. **Topic Assessments**: 20 questions after each vertical topic
3. **Section Assessments**: GMAT-format section after completing all topics
4. **Mock Simulations**: Full GMAT simulations during final preparation

## Using This Program

### For Tutors
1. Administer initial diagnostic to new students
2. Determine appropriate cycle based on results
3. Follow lesson materials in sequence
4. Assign training homework between lessons
5. Conduct topic assessments after each unit
6. Evaluate progress with section assessments
7. Advance students to next cycle when criteria are met

### For Students
1. Complete initial diagnostic honestly (no studying specifically for it)
2. Attend all scheduled lessons (90 min, 2-3x per week)
3. Complete all training assignments within time limits
4. Review mistakes thoroughly before next lesson
5. Take assessments seriously - they determine progression
