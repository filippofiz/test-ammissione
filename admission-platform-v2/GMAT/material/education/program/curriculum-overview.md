# UpToTen GMAT Preparation Program - Curriculum Overview

## Program Philosophy

The UpToTen GMAT preparation program is designed to provide structured, personalized tutoring that adapts to each student's starting level and target score. The program follows a cycle-based progression system where students advance through increasingly challenging material as they demonstrate mastery.

## Program Structure

### Section Order
The GMAT preparation follows this sequence:
1. **Quantitative Reasoning (QR)** - Mathematical foundations first
2. **Data Insights (DI)** - Builds on QR skills with data analysis
3. **Verbal Reasoning (VR)** - Last, allowing time for parallel English preparation

### Score Cycles
Students are placed into one of three cycles based on their initial placement assessment:

| Cycle | Name | Target Score | Lessons/Topic | Question Difficulty |
|-------|------|--------------|---------------|---------------------|
| 1 | Foundation | 505-605 | 4 (A, B, C, D) | 60% Easy, 30% Medium, 10% Hard |
| 2 | Development | 605-665 | 3 (A, B, C) | 25% Easy, 50% Medium, 25% Hard |
| 3 | Excellence | 665-715+ | 2 (A, B) | 5% Easy, 30% Medium, 65% Hard |

Each cycle has a decreasing number of lessons per topic, reflecting the student's increasing independence as they advance. For detailed cycle information, see [cycle-definitions.md](cycle-definitions.md).

## Vertical Lessons

Each section is divided into vertical lessons focusing on specific topics:

### Quantitative Reasoning (5 Topics)
1. Number Properties & Arithmetic
2. Algebra
3. Word Problems
4. Statistics & Probability
5. Percents, Ratios & Proportions

### Data Insights (5 Topics)
1. Data Sufficiency (DS)
2. Graphics Interpretation (GI)
3. Table Analysis (TA)
4. Two-Part Analysis (TPA)
5. Multi-Source Reasoning (MSR)

### Verbal Reasoning (2 Topics)
1. Critical Reasoning (CR)
2. Reading Comprehension (RC)

**Total: 12 Vertical Lessons**

## Lesson Flow Per Topic

The lesson structure varies by cycle. Each lesson is 90 minutes. For detailed breakdown, see [lesson-flow-guide.md](lesson-flow-guide.md).

### Cycle 1: Foundation (4 Lessons per Topic)
- **Lesson A**: Frontal lesson + Guided practice → Assign Quick Test
- **Lesson B**: Quick Test review + Deep concepts → Assign Training #1
- **Lesson C**: Training #1 review + Advanced strategies → Assign Training #2
- **Lesson D**: Training #2 review + Topic Assessment

### Cycle 2: Development (3 Lessons per Topic)
- **Lesson A**: Frontal lesson + Quick Test (in-class) → Assign Training #1
- **Lesson B**: Training #1 review + Deep dive → Assign Training #2
- **Lesson C**: Training #2 review + Topic Assessment

### Cycle 3: Excellence (2 Lessons per Topic)
- **Lesson A**: Condensed concepts + Quick Test + Strategy → Assign Training + Modeling
- **Lesson B**: Training review + Modeling review + Topic Assessment

### Autonomous Work (Between Lessons)
- **Quick Test**: 10 questions (5 practice + 5 theory), tutor-evaluated
- **Training #1 & #2**: 15-20 questions each (30-45 min, strictly timed)
- **Modeling Questions** (Excellence only): Advanced problem-solving exercises

## Assessment Checkpoints

1. **Familiarization Mock Test**: Introduction to GMAT question formats (~45 min, not scored)
2. **Placement Assessment**: Simil-GMAT test to determine starting cycle (~1.5 hrs)
3. **Quick Tests**: 10 questions after Lesson A (tutor-evaluated)
4. **Topic Assessments**: 20 questions after completing each topic
5. **Section Assessments**: GMAT-format section after completing all topics in a section
6. **Mock Simulations**: Full GMAT simulations during final preparation

For detailed assessment information, see [assessment-strategy.md](assessment-strategy.md).

## Using This Program

### For Tutors
1. Administer familiarization mock test to new students
2. Conduct placement assessment and determine appropriate cycle
3. Follow lesson materials in sequence for the assigned cycle
4. Evaluate Quick Tests after Lesson A
5. Assign and review training homework between lessons
6. Conduct topic assessments after each unit
7. Evaluate progress with section assessments
8. Advance students to next cycle when criteria are met (see [cycle-definitions.md](cycle-definitions.md))

### For Students
1. Complete familiarization mock test to understand GMAT format
2. Complete placement assessment honestly (no studying specifically for it)
3. Attend all scheduled lessons (90 min, 2-3x per week)
4. Complete Quick Tests and training assignments within time limits
5. Review mistakes thoroughly before next lesson
6. Take assessments seriously - they determine progression
