# Question Allocation Guide

## Overview

This document specifies the exact question requirements for each component of the GMAT preparation program. Use this guide when preparing materials and allocating questions from the question bank.

---

## Training Sessions

### QR - Quantitative Reasoning

| Topic | Training 1 | Training 2 | Total per Topic |
|-------|------------|------------|-----------------|
| Number Properties & Arithmetic | 18 questions | 18 questions | 36 |
| Algebra | 18 questions | 18 questions | 36 |
| Word Problems | 18 questions | 18 questions | 36 |
| Statistics & Probability | 18 questions | 18 questions | 36 |
| Percents, Ratios & Proportions | 18 questions | 18 questions | 36 |

**Format**: 18 questions, 36-40 minutes, ~2 min/question

### DI - Data Insights

| Topic | Training 1 | Training 2 | Total per Topic |
|-------|------------|------------|-----------------|
| Data Sufficiency (DS) | 14 questions | 14 questions | 28 |
| Graphics Interpretation (GI) | 14 questions | 14 questions | 28 |
| Table Analysis (TA) | 10 questions | 10 questions | 20 |

| Multi-Source Reasoning (MSR) | 8 sets | 8 sets | 16 sets |

- DS/GI: 14 questions, 35-40 min, ~2.5 min/question
- TA/TPA: 10 questions, 30-35 min, ~3 min/question
- MSR: 8 question sets, 30-35 min, ~4 min/set

### VR - Verbal Reasoning

| Topic | Training 1 | Training 2 | Total per Topic |
|-------|------------|------------|-----------------|
| Critical Reasoning (CR) | 16 questions | 16 questions | 32 |
| Reading Comprehension (RC) | 4 passages (14-16 Q) | 4 passages (14-16 Q) | ~32 |
| **VR Training Total** | ~30-32 | ~30-32 | **~64** |

**Format**:
- CR: 16 questions, 32-35 min, ~2 min/question
- RC: 4 passages with 3-4 questions each, 35-40 min, ~8 min/passage

## Notes for Question Bank Management

1. **Reserve Questions**: Keep some questions reserved for repeat assessments
2. **No Repetition**: Never reuse questions across training and assessments for same student
3. **Variety**: Ensure questions within a set cover different subtypes
4. **Progression**: Later training should include slightly harder questions
5. **Balance**: Each assessment should cover all relevant categories
