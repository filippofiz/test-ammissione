# Assessment Strategy

## Assessment Types Overview

| Assessment Type | When | Questions | Time | Adaptive | Purpose |
|----------------|------|-----------|------|----------|---------|
| Familiarization Mock | Onboarding (Day 1) | ~20 | ~45m | No | Introduce GMAT formats |
| Placement Assessment | Onboarding (Day 2-3) | 45 | ~1.5h | No | Determine starting cycle |
| Quick Tests | After Lesson A | 10 | 15-20m | No | Verify initial understanding |
| Training Sessions | Between lessons | 15-20 | 30-45m | No | Practice & skill building |
| Topic Assessments | After each topic | 20 | 40-45m | No | Verify topic mastery |
| Section Assessments | After section completion | 20-23 | 45m | No | Evaluate section readiness |
| Mock Simulations | Final preparation | 64 | 2h 15m | Yes | Full GMAT preparation |

## Initial Assessment: Two-Step Process

### Step 1: Familiarization Mock

#### Purpose
- Introduce students to all GMAT question formats
- Reduce test anxiety by removing unfamiliarity
- Prepare students for the actual Placement Assessment
- NOT used for scoring or placement

#### Format
- **Total Questions**: ~20 (representative sample)
- **Total Time**: ~45 minutes
- **Sections**:
  - Quantitative Reasoning: 6-7 questions
  - Data Insights: 6-8 questions (all types)
  - Verbal Reasoning: 6-7 questions
- **Adaptive**: No
- **Scored**: No - educational introduction only

#### Administration
1. Schedule as first assessment session
2. Tutor walks through each question type with student
3. Pause to explain formats and instructions
4. Encourage questions about the test format
5. Discuss observations and schedule Placement Assessment

#### Template
See: `assessments/initial-diagnostic/familiarization-mock.typ`

---

### Step 2: Placement Assessment

#### Purpose
- Determine student's starting cycle (Foundation / Development / Excellence)
- Identify strengths and weaknesses by section/topic
- Establish baseline for progress tracking
- Create personalized study plan

#### Format
- **Total Questions**: 45
- **Total Time**: ~1.5 hours
- **Sections**:
  - Quantitative Reasoning: 15 questions, 32 minutes
  - Data Insights: 15 questions, 32 minutes
  - Verbal Reasoning: 15 questions, 32 minutes
- **Adaptive**: No (fixed difficulty distribution)
- **Difficulty Mix**: 5 Easy + 5 Medium + 5 Hard per section

#### Scoring & Placement

| Raw Score | Percentage | Est. GMAT Score | Assigned Cycle |
|-----------|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| 0-17 | 0-38% | Below 505 | Foundation (Extended) |
| 18-25 | 40-55% | 505-605 | Foundation |
| 26-33 | 58-73% | 605-665 | Development |
| 34-45 | 76-100% | 665+ | Excellence |

#### Administration
1. Schedule 1-2 days after Familiarization Mock
2. Ensure quiet, distraction-free environment
3. Use official timing strictly
4. No breaks between sections
5. Grade immediately and determine cycle

#### Template
See: `assessments/initial-diagnostic/placement-assessment-template.typ`

## Quick Tests

### Purpose
- Verify understanding of concepts taught in Lesson A
- Identify gaps before Training #1 begins
- Combine practice questions with theory questions
- Evaluated by tutor (not auto-graded)

### Format
- **Questions**: 10 (5 practice + 5 theory)
- **Time**: 15-20 minutes
- **Practice Questions**: GMAT-style multiple choice
- **Theory Questions**: Open-ended written answers
- **Versions**: 3 versions per topic (one per cycle)

### Difficulty by Cycle

| Cycle | Practice Difficulty | Theory Difficulty |
|-------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Foundation | 70% Easy, 30% Medium | Basic definitions and concepts |
| Development | 40% Easy, 50% Medium, 10% Hard | Intermediate explanations |
| Excellence | 20% Medium, 80% Hard | Advanced proofs and derivations |

### Theory Question Examples

**Foundation:**
- "What is a prime number?"
- "Define the Greatest Common Factor (GCF)."
- "List the steps to solve a rate problem."

**Development:**
- "Explain why 1 is not considered a prime number."
- "Describe the relationship between GCF and LCM."
- "When would you use the weighted average formula?"

**Excellence:**
- "Derive the formula: GCF(a,b) x LCM(a,b) = a x b."
- "Explain the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic."
- "Compare algebraic and substitution approaches for DS questions."

### Administration by Cycle

- **Foundation**: Assigned as homework after Lesson A, reviewed in Lesson B (25 min)
- **Development**: Completed in-class during Lesson A (20 min), reviewed immediately (15 min)
- **Excellence**: Completed in-class during Lesson A (15 min), reviewed immediately (10 min)

### Evaluation Process
1. Tutor reviews both practice and theory answers
2. Practice: check correctness and approach used
3. Theory: evaluate completeness, accuracy, and understanding
4. Provide written feedback for Foundation students
5. Note gaps to address in subsequent lessons

## Training Sessions

### Purpose
- Apply concepts learned in frontal lesson
- Build speed and accuracy
- Identify individual problem patterns
- Prepare for topic assessment

### Format by Section

#### QR Training
- **Questions**: 15-20
- **Time**: 30-45 minutes
- **Pace**: ~2 minutes per question
- **Difficulty**: Cycle-appropriate

#### DI Training (varies by type)

| DI Type | Questions | Time | Notes |
|---------|-----------|------|-------|
| DS | 12-15 | 30-40 min | ~2.5 min/question |
| GI | 12-15 | 30-40 min | ~2.5 min/question |
| TA | 10-12 | 30-36 min | ~3 min/question |
| TPA | 10-12 | 30-36 min | ~3 min/question |
| MSR | 8-10 sets | 30-40 min | ~3-4 min/set |

#### VR Training

| VR Type | Questions | Time | Notes |
|---------|-----------|------|-------|
| CR | 15-18 | 30-36 min | ~2 min/question |
| RC | 3-4 passages (12-16 Q) | 30-40 min | 6-8 min/passage |

### Administration
1. Assigned at end of lesson (after Quick Test review)
2. Student completes at home before next lesson
3. **Must be strictly timed** - no pausing
4. Student records time per question if possible
5. Review results at start of next lesson

### Training Review Protocol
1. Calculate accuracy rate
2. Identify error patterns (conceptual vs. careless)
3. Note time management issues
4. Prioritize 3-5 questions for detailed review
5. Connect errors to lesson concepts

## Topic Assessments

### Purpose
- Verify mastery of specific topic
- Determine if student is ready for next topic
- Identify need for additional practice
- Contribute to cycle progression decision

### Format
- **Questions**: 20
- **Time**: 40-45 minutes
- **Difficulty**: Cycle-appropriate mix
- **Adaptive**: No (fixed question set)

### Scoring Thresholds

| Cycle | Pass | Review Needed | Repeat Topic |
|-------|------|---------------|--------------|
| Foundation | 70%+ | 60-69% | Below 60% |
| Development | 75%+ | 65-74% | Below 65% |
| Excellence | 80%+ | 70-79% | Below 70% |

### Administration by Cycle
- **Foundation (Lesson D)**: 45 min assessment, 15 min review
- **Development (Lesson C)**: 40 min assessment, 15 min review
- **Excellence (Lesson B)**: 35 min assessment, 10 min review

### Topic Assessment Review
1. Grade immediately
2. Review all incorrect answers
3. Identify pattern of errors
4. Decide: proceed to next topic or additional practice
5. Document results for cycle progression tracking

## Section Assessments

### Purpose
- Evaluate overall section readiness
- Simulate real GMAT section pressure
- Major checkpoint for cycle progression
- Prepare student for full mock simulations

### Format (matches GMAT section format)

| Section | Questions | Time |
|---------|-----------|------|
| QR | 21 | 45 minutes |
| DI | 20 | 45 minutes |
| VR | 23 | 45 minutes |

### Timing in Program
- Administered after completing all topics in a section
- Recommended: 3-4 section assessments before moving to next section
- Space assessments 1-2 weeks apart

### Scoring & Interpretation
- Calculate percentage correct
- Compare to cycle target range
- Analyze by topic within section
- Track improvement across multiple assessments

### Progression Decision Matrix

| Assessment # | Score vs Target | Action |
|--------------|-----------------|--------|
| 1st | Below target | Continue practice, schedule 2nd |
| 1st | At/above target | Schedule 2nd to confirm |
| 2nd | Below target | Identify weak areas, targeted practice |
| 2nd | At/above target | Ready for next section or mock |
| 3rd+ | Consistently below | Consider extending cycle or addressing fundamentals |

## Mock Simulations

### Purpose
- Full GMAT experience
- Build test-day stamina
- Final cycle progression checkpoint
- Identify last-minute adjustments

### Format
- **Total Questions**: 64
- **Total Time**: 2 hours 15 minutes
- **Sections**: QR (21) + DI (20) + VR (23)
- **Adaptive**: Yes
- **No breaks** between sections

### Recommended Schedule
- 3-5 full mocks during final preparation phase
- Space 1-2 weeks apart
- Alternate with targeted review sessions

### Mock Review Protocol
1. Score by section and total
2. Compare to target score
3. Analyze timing (finished early/late?)
4. Review hardest questions per section
5. Identify patterns across mocks
6. Adjust strategy as needed
